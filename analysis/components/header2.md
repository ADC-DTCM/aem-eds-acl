# header — Development-Ready Spec

> Generated by the AEM EDS Component Analyst.
> Source: `https://www.doc.govt.nz/`  ·  Component: `header`  ·  Generated: 2026-04-29

## 1. Summary

- The site header is a two-band region rendered at the top of every page: a 20 px gold accent strip stacked above an 88 px dark-green main band that holds brand, navigation, campaign badge, log-in link and search.
- Width-conditional behaviour: at viewport widths `≥ 768 px` the header exposes an inline mega-menu navigation; at viewport widths `< 768 px` it collapses every nav surface behind a hamburger trigger that opens a vertical drawer.
- Top-level navigation is partitioned into four named sections — **Parks & recreation**, **Nature**, **Get involved**, **Our work** — each with its own brand colour that propagates into the section's mega-menu and into the equivalent drawer bar.
- The component carries five distinct overlays: a per-section mega-menu panel (desktop), a search-autosuggest dropdown, a mobile drawer, a per-section in-drawer expansion, and a focus-revealed skip link.
- Audience: anonymous site visitors (primary), authenticated DOC bookings users (log-in path), keyboard and assistive-technology users (skip link, ARIA-driven menus), and authors who maintain navigation, campaign badge, and log-in destination.

## 3. Content sequences (within the `header` region)

1. **Top accent strip** — full-width, 20 px tall, solid gold band; no content, decorative only.
2. **Brand row** — left-anchored brand wordmark linking to the site root.
3. **Primary nav row (desktop, ≥ 768 px)** — four named top-level entries arranged horizontally inside a 1066 px-wide centred row; each entry is a pair of (section landing-page link) + (chevron-down trigger button).
4. **Campaign badge** — circular SVG ("Always Be Naturing") that sits to the right of the primary nav row and overflows the green band downward (133 × 129 px on a 108 px-tall band). Authoring: optional decorative link.
5. **Utility cluster** — right-anchored within the green band: a leading-icon "Log in" link followed by a search input + submit button.
6. **Mobile control row (< 768 px)** — replaces the primary nav row with: brand · log-in icon-button · hamburger trigger.
7. **Mobile search row (< 768 px)** — full-width search input + submit button stacked under the brand row.
8. **Mega-menu panel (desktop, per section)** — overlay anchored at `top: 108px`, max-width `1024 px`, white background, two horizontally-arranged sub-sequences:
   - 8a. **Section sub-list** — vertical column of full-width section-coloured chip rows (label on left, trailing arrow icon on right).
   - 8b. **Popular-chips rail** — heading `Popular` followed by a wrap of outlined chip-buttons (border-only, dark text).
9. **Mobile drawer (< 768 px)** — replaces all main content below the brand row when open; contains four stacked **section bars** (one per top-level section) coloured with that section's brand colour. Tapping a bar reveals an inline copy of sequences **8a** and **8b** under that bar.
10. **Search autosuggest panel** — overlay anchored to the search input, white background, dark text, lists 0–N matching query suggestions when the input has at least one character.
11. **Skip-to-main-content link** — visually-hidden-by-default, focus-revealed link that is the first focusable child of `<body>` and targets `#main-heading`.

## 4. Shared atoms

- **Section colour token** — every section has a single brand colour applied to (a) its mega-menu sub-list rows, (b) its mobile-drawer section bar, (c) hover/focus states of its sub-list rows. Tokens observed on the live source: Parks & recreation `rgb(21, 121, 183)` (hover `rgb(...700)`); Nature `rgb(80, 127, 57)`; Get involved `rgb(76, 54, 87)`; Our work `rgb(128, 51, 26)`. The downstream theme is free to expose these as four CSS custom properties (e.g. `--section-color-parks`, `…-nature`, `…-get-involved`, `…-our-work`).
- **Section sub-link row** — a 100 %-width inline-flex row, `padding: 6px 10px`, label on left, trailing 12 × 12 px arrow-right icon on right. Background = section colour at rest, hover = darker shade of same colour, text = white at desktop / dark on mobile drawer (white on chevron-row background, dark on white chip rows).
- **Outlined chip** — inline-flex button-like link, `border: 2px solid rgb(56, 66, 70)`, `padding: 8px 12px`, font-size `12px`, transparent background at rest. Used in the Popular rail and in the body-content quick-links area outside the header (out of scope).
- **Top-level disclosure pair** — a link (the section landing page) immediately followed by a chevron-down `<button aria-expanded aria-controls>`. The two share visual treatment (white text, 14 px) but are independently focusable.

## 6. Cross-cutting requirements

- **Performance:**
  - The header must contribute zero **CLS**: measured `headerHeight` is `108 px` at desktop and `152 px` at mobile, both fixed. Asset loading (logo, badge SVG, autosuggest panel) must reserve those heights from initial paint.
  - The brand wordmark (`148 × 56 px` SVG) and the optional campaign badge (`133 × 129 px`) must be loaded eagerly with `fetchpriority="high"` (or equivalent) — they sit above the fold on every page and are part of the LCP region.
  - The autosuggest network request (story H11) must be debounced (≥ 200 ms) and must not block primary-thread rendering.
- **Positioning / scroll:**
  - The live source uses `position: static` for the header (`position` measured `static` at all viewports). The component **must not** introduce sticky/fixed positioning unless a downstream business decision adds it explicitly.
  - With `position: static`, content scrolls **under no offset** below the header; no scroll-margin or `--header-height` token is required for in-page anchors except for the skip link target (story H14).
- **SEO / structured data:**
  - The brand link must use `<a href="…">` (not JS navigation); the four section landing-page links must remain crawlable independently of their chevron buttons (do not reuse the chevron as the only path to the section page — covered by H3).
- **Internationalisation:**
  - Text labels (`title`, `loginLabel`, `popularLabel`, `searchPlaceholder`, `skipLinkText`) are independently authorable and must accept Unicode strings of arbitrary length up to a sensible cap (256 chars) without breaking the layout. The mega-menu sub-list rows must wrap rather than truncate when the label is longer than 24 characters.
  - **RTL:** logical properties (`padding-inline`, `border-inline-start`, `inset-inline`) must be used so the header mirrors correctly under `dir="rtl"`.
- **Theming / tokens:**
  - The header is expected to consume the following CSS custom properties from `styles/styles.css`: `--background-color` (green band), `--accent-color` (gold strip), `--text-color`, `--link-color`, `--body-font-family`. Section colours should be exposed as four additional custom properties (e.g. `--section-color-parks`, `…-nature`, `…-get-involved`, `…-our-work`) so authors can re-skin sections without re-authoring the JSON model.

## 7. Out of scope

- **Footer / `<footer>` region** — the captured snapshots include footer content for context but the footer is its own component (deferred to a separate `propmts/footer-spec.md`).
- **Any block / decoration that lives below `<header>`** — the page hero, image-caption toggle (`uid=1_26`), and quick-link chip rail under the hero are not part of the header.
- **Visual styling of the search results page** — only the search **submission** is in scope (story H10); the destination page is a separate spec.
- **Analytics / tracking events fired by header interactions** — out of scope. The console errors observed during capture were not header-originated and are not regressions to fix.
